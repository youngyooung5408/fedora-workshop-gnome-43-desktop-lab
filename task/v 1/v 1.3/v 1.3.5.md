## Completed design

- Keep the live clock and market in the stable main-workspace desktop background layer.
- During Activities, place the disposable non-interactive replica under GNOME Shell's actual `Meta.BackgroundActor` for the active main workspace rather than adding it as a sibling in the background group's `Clutter.BinLayout`.
- Give the wallpaper child no expand flags. Size its frame from the wallpaper actor's current allocation, retain the source's native monitor dimensions, and use one `Math.min(width ratio, height ratio)` scale on both axes.
- Recenter and rescale on wallpaper-allocation changes so the window-picker and Show Applications states stay proportional and the clock remains circular.
- Hide both the frame and clone from Shell's all-actor drag picking, and cancel a stale custom-dock drag when Shell begins dragging a window.
- Preserve the accepted v1.3.4 clock, market, compact Show Applications, hidden bottom dash, and all unrelated desktop behavior.

## Diagnosis

GNOME Shell 49 builds each overview wallpaper with a `Meta.BackgroundGroup`
using `Clutter.BinLayout`. The v1.3.4 native-size frame was added as another
child of that group. Even though its clone used equal X/Y scaling, that sibling
could still affect only the main workspace's requested geometry, explaining why
the widget workspace and the plain neighboring workspace shrank differently.

A static wallpaper image cannot run a live clock or market. Parenting the
non-interactive replica under the wallpaper actor is the Shell equivalent of
putting it into the background: it inherits that actor's overview placement
without taking part in the workspace layout. `reactive: false` alone is not
enough for drag-and-drop because Shell deliberately picks all actors during a
window drag, so v1.3.5 also explicitly hides the replica subtree from picking.
