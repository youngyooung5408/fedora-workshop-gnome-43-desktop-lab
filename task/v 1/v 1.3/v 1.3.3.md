## Completed design

- Make the market panel exactly 25% wider than the prior responsive range and keep the add/search chooser at the same width as the market panel.
- Limit the displayed watchlist to 10 instruments, preserve the 10 most recently added records when reading an older oversized configuration, and show up to six rows in a fixed scrolling list.
- Display the latest available amount with its currency. Use the official TWSE market-information latest trade for TWSE/TPEx instruments, fall back to the official closing feed and cached value, and use Twelve Data's latest-price endpoint for international instruments after the owner supplies a key.
- Show `Key needed` for an international or unresolved legacy symbol that cannot be priced without the optional Twelve Data key, instead of leaving an unexplained `--`.
- Put the Activities replica in GNOME Shell 49's full-monitor overview wallpaper group rather than its work-area bin. The replica and wallpaper therefore receive the same aspect ratio, clipping, and zoom transform while the live desktop actor remains stable.
- Remove the nested scrollbar from running applications, keep their section at the bottom of the left dock, and restore one compact Show Applications button above that section without restoring GNOME's bottom overview dash.
- Dismiss the dock editor and the market edit/add interaction when a click lands outside their panels, matching the accepted click-outside behavior of app-cluster flyouts.

## Current-price answer

The v1.3.2 helper did fetch prices, but the saved watchlist begins with several
legacy international symbols and this VM has no Twelve Data key. Those rows had
no price and stayed as `--`; the valid Taiwan rows were lower in an unbounded
list. v1.3.3 makes that state explicit, bounds and scrolls the list, and uses
latest-price sources for configured instruments.

## Wallpaper model

GNOME's ordinary wallpaper setting is a static image and cannot itself contain
live interactive clock or market code. The supported extension approximation
keeps one live desktop actor and paints a non-interactive replica inside
Activities' own full-monitor wallpaper group. This gives the requested visual
attachment and native zoom behavior without pretending the bitmap file is
dynamic or reparenting a live actor during an overview transition.
